About gerbils

In burrows deep, where shadows play,
Tiny whiskered creatures, in fur array.
Gerbils, a dance of nature's grace,
A world in miniature, a bustling place.

With fur of silk, a sandy hue,
They scurry and scamper, a joy to view.
Eyes like beads, so bright and keen,
In their world, a kingdom unseen.

Cheeks that pouch a secret stash,
Gnawing teeth that never clash.
A ballet of tails in rhythmic delight,
Gerbils dance through the soft moonlight.

In tunnels woven, a labyrinth spun,
A web of life, their haven won.
Tiny architects, builders so sly,
Crafting homes 'neath the open sky.

Oh, gerbils, guardians of the earth,
In your realm, we find rebirth.
A lesson learned in the smallest of paws,
Nature's poetry, without a pause.

Gentle souls with hearts so pure,
In their eyes, an innocence to endure.
Gerbils, a symphony of whiskers and fur,
A testament to life's soft, rhythmic blur.
